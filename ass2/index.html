Car Crashes Statistics In Australia

Total Fatalities Per State

Observing the data shown by the map, western side states prove to have more fatalities when it comes to road incidents.
A large reason why this is the case is because of the denser populations in these regions compared to the other states.
Which follows through on current estimates based on population.gov.au (NSW: 8,189,266, VIC: 6,649,159, QLD: 5,221,170.)
This also allows a conlusion to made that denser populations lead to greater fatalities or proportionate to the population.




Total Fatalities Per Month

The heat map indicates the spread of monthly fatalities in a year. It can be observed that during the quartely holiday periods greater fatalities occur.
This is expected as roads become more dense and there is higher probability that these festive periods have drug influenced accidents as well such as alcohol and other substances.
However, further looking at the time-series we can months have also began to shift in total crashes as well.
We can see this in October and November from 1989 to 1990 in which October became less incident prone after the years which indicate that policies in road safety or general safety was improving.
